High-quality Speech Synthesis Using Mel Spectrogram Super-resolution

Authors: Leyuan Sheng, Evgeniy N. Pavlovskiy

Abstract: In speech synthesis and enhancement systems, we are required to precisely generate mel spectrograms, which have been widely used as key acoustic representations. However, the generated spectrograms have oversmoothness and are less detailed than the ground truth. To overcome these limitations, we are motivated by the success of image-to-image translation, combining the strengths of pix2pixHD and ResUnet to propose a learning-based postfilter, which useing super-resolution to reconstruct the mel spectrograms for high-quality speech synthesis. Firstly from the synthesized speech, we extract the coarse mel spectrogram as baseline compared with the original mel spectrogram. We then using spectrogram super-resolution network to predict the enhanced spectrogram, followed by Griffin-Lim and a pre-trained WaveNet model acting as the vocoder to synthesize speech from those spectrograms. Mean Opinion Scores show that predicted mel spectrogram achieves 3.73 (Griffin-Lim) and 4.01 (WaveNet), has a good improvement (Griffin-Lim: 0.44, WaveNet: 0.17) over the baseline, comparable to 3.74 (Griffin-Lim) and 4.04 (WaveNet) for original mel spectrogram. Also the predicted mel spectrogram using Griffin-Lim achieves 3.73, which is close to 3.84 for coarse mel spectrogram using WaveNet. Finally, as far as we know, we present the first truly combined spectrogram processing with image-to-image translation.
